Reducing Food Waste: Simple Steps

Food waste is a big issue, but there are easy ways to make a difference right at home. By being more mindful of how we buy, store, and use food, we can save money and help the planet.

Plan Your Meals

Before you shop, take a few minutes to plan your meals for the week. This helps you buy only what you need and avoids impulse purchases that might go bad.

Smart Storage

Knowing how to store different foods can extend their life. For example, some fruits and vegetables last longer in the fridge, while others prefer the counter. Look up storage tips for specific items.

Understand Dates

"Best before" and "use by" dates mean different things. "Best before" is about quality, so food might still be safe to eat after this date. "Use by" is about safety, so it's best to eat or freeze food before this date.

Love Your Leftovers

Don't let leftovers go to waste! Get creative and turn them into new meals, or simply enjoy them the next day for lunch. Proper storage in airtight containers keeps them fresh.

Compost When Possible

Even with our best efforts, some food waste is unavoidable. Composting food scraps like fruit peels and vegetable trimmings helps return nutrients to the soil and reduces landfill waste.
